Protection services work best when the first month is handled correctly.

Many early issues people blame on "bad products" are actually aftercare mistakes in the first week.

> **Quick answer:** Give the materials time to cure, avoid aggressive washing early, and follow a simple staged routine for the first 30 days.

---

## Day 0-2: Let Everything Settle

### Do

- Park in a clean, shaded area when possible.
- Let installers' curing guidance lead your routine.
- Expect minor temporary visual effects (light moisture haze, small edges settling).

### Avoid

- Pressure washing.
- Scrubbing film/tint edges.
- Chemical cleaners.
- Testing edges with fingernails or tools.

This period is about stability, not appearance perfection.

---

## Day 3-7: Gentle Care Only

### PPF

- Light hand wash only if needed.
- Keep pressure away from edges and seams.
- Avoid aggressive bug/tar chemicals on fresh film.

### Window Tinting

- Keep windows up for the recommended period.
- Do not clean interior glass until cure guidance allows it.
- Do not use ammonia-based cleaners.

### Ceramic Coating

- Avoid detergent-heavy washing.
- Remove obvious bird droppings quickly but gently.
- Do not apply extra toppers unless advised.

---

## Week 2: Controlled Normalization

At this stage, most cars can return to a normal but careful routine:

- pH-neutral shampoo
- clean wash media
- soft drying towel or filtered air
- ammonia-free glass cleaner for tinted glass

If you see anything unusual, ask your installer early rather than experimenting.

---

## Week 3-4: Build the Long-Term Routine

By this point, your maintenance pattern matters more than cure sensitivity.

### Recommended baseline

- Wash every 1-2 weeks (depending on usage).
- Quick contaminant removal between washes.
- Keep tools clean to prevent adding fresh marring.

### Monthly checks

- PPF edge condition and contamination.
- Tint clarity and interior haze status.
- Coating water behavior (beading/sheeting consistency).

---

## Service-Specific Aftercare Summary

| Service | Early Priority | Biggest Mistake |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| PPF | Protect edges during early cure | Direct high-pressure at edges too soon |
| Window tinting | Let interior film cure undisturbed | Cleaning too early with wrong chemical |
| Ceramic coating | Gentle wash chemistry | Harsh wash products in first weeks |

---

## Red Flags: When to Contact Your Installer

- persistent large bubbles after expected cure window
- edge lifting that increases over days
- unusual haze that does not improve
- patchy behavior after first proper wash

Early review usually makes correction easier and cleaner.

---

## A Smart First-Month Bundle

For most owners:

1. Install protection service.
2. Follow strict week-1 aftercare.
3. Book a structured check-up wash in week 3 or 4.

That final check helps verify the finish and locks in a stable maintenance routine.
