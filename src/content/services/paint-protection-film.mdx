  - question: "Do bubbles or haze after install mean it’s a bad job?"
    answer: "A small amount of trapped moisture or light silvering can appear in the first 24–72 hours and dissipates as the film breathes and cures.   - question: "Can seams be made invisible?"
    answer: "Mid‑panel seams are often visible—especially on light colors. We use full‑panel wraps or hide seams on panel edges whenever possible. Attempting to ‘melt’ seams together is not advised and can damage the film."
  - question: "Why is PPF more expensive than ceramic coating?"
    answer: "PPF is a thick, impact‑absorbing film that requires precision patterning, stretching, and edge work—significantly more material and labor than liquid coatings. Installer skill greatly affects the final result; we prioritize fitment, edges, and finish over speed."
  - question: "Can I add ceramic coating over PPF?"
    answer: "Yes, and we recommend it. Ceramic over PPF adds hydrophobics, boosts gloss, and makes cleaning easier. It does not replace PPF’s impact protection; they complement each other."
  - question: "How do I care for PPF to avoid issues?"
    answer: "Hand‑wash with pH‑neutral soap, avoid abrasive brushes, and promptly remove contaminants. In the first week, avoid pressure‑washing edges while the film fully cures. Regular washing reduces sand build‑up that can cause micro‑marring."
